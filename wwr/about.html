History

Founded in 1985, Rapids Edge Expeditions started as a small group of adrenaline seekers exploring the rugged rivers of Colorado. What began as a weekend passion quickly turned into a mission—to bring the thrill of white-water rafting to adventurers worldwide. Armed with just three rafts and a deep love for the outdoors, founders Tom Caldwell and Lisa Rivers guided their first customers down the roaring rapids of the Arkansas River. Word spread quickly, and within five years, the company expanded to offer guided rafting trips in the Grand Canyon and the Snake River. With an emphasis on safety, environmental conservation, and unforgettable experiences, Rapids Edge Expeditions built a reputation as the premier rafting outfitter in the United States. By the early 1990s, the company introduced multi-day rafting tours, offering thrill-seekers an immersive wilderness experience, complete with riverside camping and expert storytelling around the fire.

As Rapids Edge Expeditions grew, so did its ambition. In the early 2000s, the company expanded beyond U.S. borders, leading rafting expeditions in Costa Rica, New Zealand, and the Zambezi River in Africa. The introduction of customized adventure packages, from family-friendly floats to extreme Class V rapids, made white-water rafting accessible to everyone. By 2010, the company had invested in eco-friendly rafting technology, using sustainable rafts and solar-powered base camps. This commitment to conservation helped Rapids Edge Expeditions partner with national parks and wildlife organizations to preserve river ecosystems. Today, the company stands as a global leader in white-water rafting, known for its expert guides, world-class safety measures, and dedication to adventure tourism. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned rafter, Rapids Edge Expeditions promises an unforgettable ride through some of the world's most breathtaking rapids, continuing its legacy of adventure and conservation
